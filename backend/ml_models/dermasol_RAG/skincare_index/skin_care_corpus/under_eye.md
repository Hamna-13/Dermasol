# Under-Eye Care Skincare Guide

## Understanding Under-Eye Concerns

The under-eye area has thinner skin compared to the rest of the face. It contains fewer oil glands and less collagen, making it more prone to:

- Dark circles
- Puffiness
- Fine lines
- Wrinkles
- Dryness
- Visible blood vessels

Under-eye concerns may be caused by:

- Genetics
- Sleep deprivation
- Hyperpigmentation
- Sun exposure
- Aging and collagen loss
- Fluid retention
- Allergies
- Dehydration

Different types of dark circles require different approaches.

---

## Types of Under-Eye Dark Circles

### Pigmented Dark Circles
Brown or dark discoloration caused by excess melanin. Common in medium to deeper skin tones.

### Vascular Dark Circles
Blue, purple, or reddish tone caused by visible blood vessels under thin skin.

### Structural Shadowing
Hollows under the eyes that create shadow effects due to volume loss.

Skincare is most effective for:
- Pigmentation
- Mild puffiness
- Early fine lines
- Dehydration-related darkening

Structural concerns may require professional treatment.

---

## Under-Eye Care: Normal Skin

### Routine Guide

Morning:
1. Gentle cleanse
2. Lightweight eye serum
3. Moisturiser
4. SPF (including under-eye area)

Night:
1. Cleanse
2. Targeted eye treatment
3. Moisturiser

### Recommended Ingredients

- **Vitamin C (gentle formulations)** – Improves pigmentation  
- **Niacinamide** – Supports barrier and reduces discoloration  
- **Peptides** – Improve firmness  
- **Hyaluronic Acid** – Hydrates fine lines  

---

## Under-Eye Care: Pigmentation-Focused

Best for brown dark circles or post-inflammatory pigmentation.

### Recommended Ingredients

- **Vitamin C**
- **Niacinamide**
- **Alpha Arbutin**
- **Azelaic Acid (low strength, carefully applied)**

Daily sunscreen is essential to prevent worsening pigmentation.

---

## Under-Eye Care: Puffiness

Puffiness is often caused by fluid retention or poor circulation.

### Routine Guide

Morning:
1. Gentle cleanse
2. Caffeine-based eye serum
3. Light moisturiser
4. SPF

### Recommended Ingredients

- **Caffeine** – Temporarily reduces puffiness  
- **Green Tea Extract** – Antioxidant and soothing  
- **Peptides** – Improve firmness  

Cold compresses may help temporarily reduce swelling.

---

## Under-Eye Care: Fine Lines & Aging

Fine lines appear due to dehydration and collagen loss.

### Routine Guide

Night:
1. Gentle cleanse
2. Low-strength retinol (formulated for eye area)
3. Barrier moisturiser

### Recommended Ingredients

- **Retinol (low strength)** – Stimulates collagen  
- **Peptides**
- **Hyaluronic Acid**
- **Ceramides**

Introduce retinol slowly (1–2 times weekly initially).

---

## Under-Eye Care: Sensitive Skin

The under-eye area is naturally sensitive.

### Routine Guide

- Use fragrance-free products
- Apply small amount
- Avoid strong acids
- Avoid high-strength retinol

### Recommended Ingredients

- **Panthenol**
- **Ceramides**
- **Centella Asiatica**
- **Colloidal Oatmeal**

Patch testing is recommended.

---

## Core Under-Eye Ingredients Explained

### Vitamin C
Improves pigmentation and supports collagen production.

### Niacinamide
Reduces discoloration and strengthens barrier.

### Caffeine
Temporarily reduces puffiness by constricting blood vessels.

### Retinol
Stimulates collagen production and reduces fine lines when used cautiously.

### Peptides
Support firmness and elasticity.

### Hyaluronic Acid
Hydrates and reduces the appearance of fine lines caused by dryness.

---

## Sunscreen and Under-Eye Protection

UV exposure worsens dark circles and accelerates aging.  
Use sunscreen carefully under the eyes or wear sunglasses for protection.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Using face-strength retinol under the eyes  
- Applying too much product  
- Pulling or rubbing skin  
- Expecting permanent correction of genetic dark circles  
- Ignoring sleep and hydration  

---

## Realistic Expectations

- Puffiness: temporary improvement within hours  
- Pigmentation: 8–12 weeks with consistent use  
- Fine lines: 8–12 weeks with retinol  
- Structural hollows: skincare has limited effect  

Consistency and sun protection are essential.

---

## Quick Under-Eye Strategy

Morning:
Caffeine or Vitamin C eye serum → Moisturiser → SPF

Night:
Peptide or low-strength retinol → Barrier cream

Use gentle application and avoid aggressive layering.
