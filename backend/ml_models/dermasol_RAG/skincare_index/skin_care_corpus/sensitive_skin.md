# Sensitive Skin Skincare Guide

## What Is Sensitive Skin?

Sensitive skin is not a medical diagnosis but a skin condition characterized by heightened reactivity. It tends to react easily to environmental factors, skincare products, temperature changes, or friction.

Common symptoms include:

- Redness
- Burning or stinging sensation
- Itching
- Tightness
- Dry patches
- Irritation after applying products

Sensitive skin may overlap with conditions such as rosacea, eczema, or contact dermatitis. However, many individuals experience mild sensitivity without a medical condition.

The primary goal of sensitive skin care is to:

- Reduce inflammation
- Strengthen the skin barrier
- Avoid triggers
- Maintain hydration
- Prevent irritation

Minimalism and consistency are essential.

---

## Causes of Skin Sensitivity

Sensitive skin can be triggered by:

- Harsh cleansers
- Over-exfoliation
- High-strength acids
- Retinoids (especially when introduced too quickly)
- Fragrance and essential oils
- Environmental stress (sun, wind, pollution)
- Compromised skin barrier

Barrier repair is often the first step in managing sensitivity.

---

## Sensitive Skin: Normal to Combination Skin

### Routine Guide

Morning:
1. Gentle, low-pH cleanser
2. Soothing serum (Niacinamide 2–5% or Panthenol)
3. Lightweight moisturiser
4. Mineral or broad-spectrum SPF 30+

Night:
1. Gentle cleanser
2. Barrier-support serum
3. Moisturiser

Avoid introducing multiple active ingredients at once.

### Recommended Ingredients

- **Niacinamide (low to moderate strength)** – Reduces redness and strengthens barrier  
- **Panthenol (Vitamin B5)** – Soothes and hydrates  
- **Centella Asiatica** – Calms irritation  
- **Hyaluronic Acid** – Hydrates gently  
- **Ceramides** – Repair barrier  

---

## Sensitive Skin: Dry Skin

Dry sensitive skin requires both calming and strong barrier support.

### Routine Guide

Morning:
1. Cream cleanser (non-foaming)
2. Hydrating serum
3. Ceramide-rich moisturiser
4. SPF 30+

Night:
1. Gentle cleanser
2. Soothing serum
3. Barrier repair cream

Avoid daily exfoliation.

### Recommended Ingredients

- **Ceramides**
- **Cholesterol**
- **Fatty Acids**
- **Colloidal Oatmeal**
- **Squalane**
- **Panthenol**

Avoid high-strength vitamin C (L-ascorbic acid) if irritation occurs.

---

## Sensitive Skin: Oily or Acne-Prone Skin

Oily sensitive skin can be challenging because aggressive acne treatments often worsen irritation.

### Routine Guide

Morning:
1. Gentle gel cleanser
2. Niacinamide serum
3. Lightweight moisturiser
4. Non-comedogenic SPF

Night:
1. Gentle cleanser
2. Azelaic Acid (low strength, if tolerated)
3. Moisturiser

Introduce exfoliants slowly (1–2 times per week).

### Recommended Ingredients

- **Azelaic Acid (low strength)** – Anti-inflammatory and pigment-friendly  
- **Niacinamide**
- **Zinc PCA**
- **Centella Asiatica**

Avoid alcohol-heavy toners and harsh scrubs.

---

## Core Ingredients for Sensitive Skin

### Ceramides
Restore lipid barrier and reduce water loss.

### Niacinamide
Improves barrier function and reduces redness when used at moderate concentrations.

### Panthenol
Supports skin repair and reduces irritation.

### Centella Asiatica
Calms inflammation and supports healing.

### Colloidal Oatmeal
Reduces itching and irritation.

### Azelaic Acid
Well tolerated by many sensitive individuals and reduces inflammation.

---

## Ingredients to Use With Caution

Sensitive skin may react to:

- High-strength AHAs or BHAs
- High-concentration retinoids
- Strong fragrance
- Essential oils
- Alcohol-heavy products

Patch testing new products is recommended.

---

## How to Introduce Active Ingredients Safely

If adding retinol or acids:

- Start once weekly
- Apply a moisturiser before and after active (buffer method)
- Increase frequency gradually
- Stop immediately if burning or severe irritation occurs

---

## Common Mistakes

- Over-layering products  
- Switching routines too frequently  
- Ignoring sunscreen  
- Using exfoliating scrubs  
- Treating irritation with more active ingredients  

Sensitive skin improves with simplicity.

---

## When to Seek Medical Advice

If sensitivity includes:

- Persistent redness
- Severe burning
- Swelling
- Rash
- Scaling that does not improve

A dermatologist should evaluate for rosacea, dermatitis, or eczema.

---

## Quick Sensitive Skin Strategy

Morning:
Gentle cleanse → Soothing serum → Moisturiser → SPF

Night:
Gentle cleanse → Barrier cream

Keep routine minimal. Repair first, treat later.
