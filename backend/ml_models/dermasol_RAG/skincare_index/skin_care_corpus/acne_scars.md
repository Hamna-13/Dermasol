# Acne Scars & Post-Acne Marks Skincare Guide

## What Are Acne Scars?

Acne scars are long-term skin changes that remain after acne lesions heal. They can appear as:

1. **Post-Inflammatory Hyperpigmentation (PIH)** – Dark brown or red marks left after pimples heal (common in medium to deeper skin tones).
2. **Post-Inflammatory Erythema (PIE)** – Pink or red marks caused by damaged capillaries.
3. **Atrophic (Depressed) Scars** – Indented scars such as ice-pick, boxcar, or rolling scars.
4. **Hypertrophic Scars** – Raised scars (less common on the face).

Topical skincare is most effective for:
- Dark marks (PIH)
- Mild texture irregularities
- Early post-acne discoloration

Deep or indented scars may require professional procedures.

---

## Acne Marks vs Acne Scars

It is important to differentiate:

- **Dark Marks (PIH)** = excess pigment → treatable with brightening ingredients  
- **Red Marks (PIE)** = vascular redness → soothe and protect  
- **Indented Scars** = collagen loss → require collagen stimulation or procedures  

Most users searching “acne scars” are referring to dark marks.

## Acne Scars: Normal Skin

### Routine Guide

Morning:
1. Gentle cleanser
2. Vitamin C serum
3. Lightweight moisturiser
4. SPF 30+ sunscreen

Night:
1. Cleanser
2. Targeted pigment serum (Azelaic Acid / Alpha Arbutin)
3. Retinoid (2–4 times weekly if tolerated)
4. Moisturiser

### Recommended Ingredients

- **Vitamin C** – Improves discoloration and supports collagen  
- **Azelaic Acid** – Reduces post-acne pigmentation  
- **Alpha Arbutin** – Fades dark marks  
- **Retinol** – Encourages collagen production and cell turnover  

### Product Types

- Brightening serum  
- Pigment-correcting serum  
- Retinol cream  
- Broad-spectrum sunscreen  

---

## Acne Scars: Oily / Acne-Prone Skin

### Routine Guide

Morning:
1. Salicylic acid cleanser
2. Niacinamide serum
3. Lightweight moisturiser
4. SPF 50+

Night:
1. Gentle cleanser
2. Azelaic Acid treatment
3. Retinoid (if no active irritation)
4. Oil-free moisturiser

### Recommended Ingredients

- **Azelaic Acid (10–15%)** – Improves pigmentation and reduces inflammation  
- **Niacinamide (4–5%)** – Reduces pigment transfer and redness  
- **Retinol** – Stimulates collagen and improves texture  
- **Salicylic Acid** – Prevents new breakouts that worsen scars  

### Avoid

- Over-exfoliating with strong acids  
- Heavy occlusive creams  

### Product Types

- Gel-based serums  
- Oil-free retinoid  
- Matte sunscreen  

---

## Acne Scars: Dry / Sensitive Skin

### Routine Guide

Morning:
1. Cream cleanser
2. Gentle Vitamin C derivative
3. Hydrating moisturiser
4. SPF 30+

Night:
1. Cleanser
2. Low-strength Azelaic Acid
3. Barrier-support cream

Introduce retinoids slowly (1–2 times weekly).

### Recommended Ingredients

- **Azelaic Acid (low %)**
- **Niacinamide**
- **Ceramides**
- **Peptides** – Support skin repair and collagen  

### Avoid

- High-strength glycolic acid  
- Daily strong exfoliation  
- Combining too many actives  

### Product Types

- Barrier-repair cream  
- Gentle brightening serum  
- Mineral sunscreen for sensitive skin

## Core Ingredients for Acne Scars

### Vitamin C
Improves uneven tone and supports collagen production, helping fade dark marks over time.

### Azelaic Acid
Targets inflammation and pigmentation, making it useful for post-acne marks.

### Retinol
Increases cell turnover and collagen synthesis, improving texture and mild depressed scars.

### Alpha Arbutin
Inhibits melanin formation and gradually fades pigmentation.

### Niacinamide
Supports barrier repair and reduces post-inflammatory discoloration.

### Peptides
Encourage collagen production and improve mild textural irregularities.

---

## Role of Sunscreen

UV exposure worsens post-acne pigmentation and slows fading.  
Daily broad-spectrum SPF is essential when treating acne scars.

---

## How Long Does It Take?

- Post-acne dark marks: 8–12 weeks with consistent treatment  
- Mild textural scars: several months with retinoids  
- Deep indented scars: may require professional treatments (microneedling, lasers, etc.)

Consistency is more important than intensity.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Picking or squeezing acne (increases scarring risk)  
- Skipping sunscreen  
- Using strong exfoliants daily  
- Expecting overnight results  

---

## Quick Acne Scar Strategy

Morning:
Vitamin C → Moisturiser → SPF

Night:
Azelaic Acid or Alpha Arbutin → Retinoid (if tolerated) → Barrier cream

Focus on preventing new acne while treating existing marks.
