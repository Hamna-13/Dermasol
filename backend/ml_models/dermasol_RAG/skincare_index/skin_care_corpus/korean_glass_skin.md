# Korean Glass Skin Care Guide

## What Is "Glass Skin"?

“Glass skin” is a term used to describe skin that appears:

- Smooth
- Clear
- Even-toned
- Deeply hydrated
- Luminous and reflective

It does not mean lighter skin or artificially shiny skin. Instead, it refers to:

- Strong skin barrier
- Consistent hydration
- Refined texture
- Minimal visible pores
- Balanced oil production

Glass skin is achieved through long-term skin health, not quick fixes.

---

## Core Principles of Glass Skin

1. Gentle cleansing
2. Deep hydration layering
3. Barrier strengthening
4. Controlled exfoliation
5. Daily sun protection
6. Consistency

The focus is on skin health rather than aggressive correction.

---

## Step-by-Step Glass Skin Routine

### Morning Routine

1. Gentle cleanser
2. Hydrating toner or essence
3. Vitamin C serum (optional for brightness)
4. Lightweight moisturiser
5. Broad-spectrum SPF 30+

### Night Routine

1. Cleanser (double cleanse if wearing makeup)
2. Hydrating toner or essence
3. Targeted treatment (retinol or niacinamide if needed)
4. Moisturiser
5. Optional sleeping mask (1–2 times weekly)

Layer thin, lightweight products rather than one heavy cream.

---

## Glass Skin for Oily or Combination Skin

Goal: Balance oil while maintaining hydration.

### Recommended Ingredients

- **Niacinamide (4–5%)** – Controls oil and improves tone  
- **Hyaluronic Acid** – Hydrates without heaviness  
- **Green Tea Extract** – Soothing antioxidant  
- **Lightweight Vitamin C** – Enhances radiance  

Avoid heavy occlusive creams.

Focus on water-based hydration and oil balance.

---

## Glass Skin for Dry Skin

Goal: Deep hydration and barrier repair.

### Recommended Ingredients

- **Hyaluronic Acid**
- **Glycerin**
- **Ceramides**
- **Squalane**
- **Panthenol**

Use richer moisturisers and occasional sleeping masks to seal moisture.

Hydration layering is essential.

---

## Glass Skin for Sensitive Skin

Goal: Calm, strengthen, hydrate.

### Recommended Ingredients

- **Centella Asiatica**
- **Panthenol**
- **Ceramides**
- **Niacinamide (low concentration)**

Avoid strong exfoliants and high-strength acids.

Focus on barrier repair first.

---

## Controlled Exfoliation

Smooth texture is key for glass skin appearance.

Use:

- Mild AHA (1–2 times weekly)
- Low-strength BHA for oily skin
- PHA for sensitive skin

Avoid daily harsh exfoliation.

Over-exfoliation damages barrier and reduces glow.

---

## Core Ingredients for Glass Skin

### Hyaluronic Acid
Provides deep hydration and plumpness.

### Niacinamide
Improves tone, reduces pores, and strengthens barrier.

### Ceramides
Maintain healthy barrier for smooth surface.

### Vitamin C
Boosts brightness and protects against oxidative damage.

### Panthenol
Calms and supports skin repair.

---

## The Role of Sunscreen

Sun exposure reduces collagen and increases pigmentation, which prevents smooth luminous skin.

Daily SPF is essential for maintaining glow and preventing dullness.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Over-layering too many active ingredients  
- Over-exfoliating  
- Skipping sunscreen  
- Using heavy oils on oily skin  
- Expecting instant transformation  

Glass skin develops over time with consistent routine.

---

## Realistic Timeline

- Hydration glow: 2–4 weeks  
- Texture refinement: 4–8 weeks  
- Visible brightness improvement: 8–12 weeks  

Consistency is more important than complexity.

---

## Quick Glass Skin Strategy

Morning:
Hydrating toner → Lightweight serum → Moisturiser → SPF

Night:
Hydrating essence → Targeted treatment → Barrier cream

Focus on hydration, balance, and protection.
